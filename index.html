About Me

"Without data, you're just another person with an opinion."

Hi, my name is Nicola and I'm a 3+ years experienced data scientist working for ION Analytics.
I've always had a passion for logic and math from a young age and enjoyed solving puzzles. My critical thinking and problem solving skills still help me face and overcome new data challenges, especially when logic plays a key role in it and I can experiment different approaches.

Since I joined ION Analytics, my expertise has focused on financial data, both structured and unstructured, but I've also being dealing with biochemical, physical and banking data.

As someone who is passionate about data, I have a strong conviction in the advancement of scientific knowledge, and I thoroughly enjoy being introduced to the latest technologies. I enjoy brainstorming creative solutions and tend to have an analytical, confident, and ambitious mindset. I am accustomed to spending extended periods of time alone, engaging in introspective thinking. I'm very logical and rational. Basically, I am an Intellectual (INTJ).

education and milestones

2015-2018: Bachelor's degree in mathematics

In September 2018, I completed my bachelor's degree in Mathematics at the Catholic University of Brescia (IT) and received a grade of 110L/110. In these years, I deepened my logical-mathematical and statistical knowledge and started to code in Python.

2018-2020: Master's degree in physics (data science)

In December 2020, I completed my master's degree in Physics (curriculum in Data Science) at the Catholic University of Brescia and received a grade of 110L/110. In particular, for the thesis, I built a deep learning model able to predict sub-atomic particles' trajectories following a proton-proton collision.

2021-2022: data science researcher

In June 2021, I started working with the computer science team at the Catholic University of Brescia after winning a research grant.I conducted research with the aim of mitigating Bias and ensuring Fairness in supervised machine learning models. As a result of this work, a research paper was published. In addition, I've also created a fully explainable regression model capable of determining the ease of synthesizing some drugs, to be inserted in a biochemical pipeline.

2022-Present: data scientist

Since joining ION Analytics in July 2022, I have been involved in automating repetitive human tasks by constructing and training NLP models that can identify specific entities of corporate interest from texts. Additionally, I have established and continue to maintain a well-structured People's Database through the collection, transformation, and ingestion of data. Finally, I generate periodic visual and numerical reports that are presented to the executive team.
